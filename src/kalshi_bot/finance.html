The Rules — Read This When You're Tempted to Spend

  1. The bot WILL stop working eventually. Every trading edge has a lifespan. Market structure changes, competitors copy your strategy, Kalshi changes fees. Treat every profitable day as a gift, not a guarantee.
  2. Build 6 months of expenses in a savings account FIRST. Before you buy anything, before you upgrade anything. At $2K/month expenses, that's $12K. This is your "bot died" insurance. Do not touch it.
  3. Pay estimated taxes quarterly. April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15. Set aside 30% of every withdrawal immediately. The IRS will penalize you if you don't pay quarterly.
  4. Only spend money you've already withdrawn. Your Kalshi balance is not your money until it's in your bank account. Paper gains mean nothing.
  5. Keep your day job until you have 12 months of savings. Even if the bot makes 10x your salary. The bot could break during a Kalshi outage or API change. Your job is your backup.
  6. Never increase your lifestyle based on projected income. Don't sign a lease you can only afford with the bot running. Don't finance a car. Live on your pre-bot budget + 20% until you have proof of 3+ months sustained income.
  7. The compound math is seductive but fragile. "$10 to $1M in 60 days" assumes zero bad days, zero API outages, zero market changes, and perfect reinvestment. Reality has friction. Plan for half the projected return.
  8. Withdraw profits regularly. Once past your emergency fund, pull out 50% of weekly profits. Real money in a real bank account. The rest can compound in the bot.
  9. Track everything. Know exactly how much you put in, how much you took out, and what your net profit is. You'll need this for taxes and for your own sanity.
  10. Get a CPA at $5K cumulative profit. They'll cost $300-500 and save you from making a $5,000 mistake on your taxes. Worth it.
