# Independent Audit — Kalshi V2 (2026-05-28)

Forensic review of trades, backtester validity, and strategy. Cross-references git
history, the live `trades.db` (1.39 GB, 1,578 trades), and the backtest data
(`/root/kalshi-backtest/data/merged_backtest.db`). Every number below is computed
from the databases, not from the project docs.

## CORRECTION (2026-05-30, from code investigation)

Two claims in §3a below were **overstated** and are corrected here:

1. **"Implied asks are corruption" — WRONG.** `ask = 1 − opposite_bid` is *correct
   Kalshi binary mechanics* (one book; buying YES @40¢ = selling NO @60¢), not a bug.
   The real defect is only the *crossed* subset (`yes_bid + no_bid > 1`) from stale
   resting levels.
2. **"The recorder still logs crossed books" — WRONG.** The May-23 inline-trim fix
   (`60dee18`) *worked*. The bot's own `trades.db` recording: window_snapshots went
   82.8% crossed (pre-fix) → **0.43%** (post-fix); orderbook_snapshots 73.2% → **0.36%**.
   Current live/paper recording is clean.

**What IS still true and damning:** the backtest dataset `merged_backtest.db` is
**stale pre-fix data** — 60% (Apr) / 78% (May) crossed in the trading window — and was
never regenerated from clean recordings. Every published backtest number ran on it.
So the backtest *conclusions* remain invalid, but the fix is "rebuild the dataset from
post-May-24 clean data," not "fix the recorder." The flex-engine look-ahead (§3b)
is independent of data and still stands. Net: the report's verdict is unchanged; the
remediation in rec #2 is corrected (see below).

## BOTTOM LINE

**The profitability this project believes in does not exist in the real-money data.**
The headline "NO side +$460, 75.5% WR — the entire profit engine" (HANDOFF.md /
CLAUDE.md) is **paper trading mislabeled as live.** Real live results:

| Scope | Trades | WR | Net PnL |
|---|---|---|---|
| **All live, all-time** | ~1,007 | ~9% | **−$73.58** |
| Live NO side (the "profit engine") | 115 | 25.2% | **−$17.51** |
| Live YES side | 892* | 5.0% | **−$56.07** |
| **PAPER NO side (what was reported as "live")** | 370 | 72.2% | **+$576** |
| Clean-era live (May, momentum only) | 128 | 39% | **−$11.02** |
| Same period, PAPER | 495 | 57% | **+$544** |

\*YES count is inflated by 807 broken April `lwm` trades — see timeline.

Three independent problems each invalidate the success story:
1. **Paper ≠ live, and "paper" was reported as "live."** The +$460 engine is simulated.
2. **The backtest is built on ~62% crossed-book corrupted data** (both engines).
3. **The flex backtester has look-ahead bias + a fabricated payoff** on top of #2.

The faint thing that *is* real — live `time_exit` is marginally positive (+$18 over
34 trades) — is within statistical noise and is erased by settlement leakage.

## 1. The "+$460 profit engine" is paper, mislabeled as live

CLAUDE.md / HANDOFF.md state, as the central finding driving all strategy decisions:
> "NO side: 75.5% WR, +$460.48 — the entire profit engine. YES side: 34.2% WR, −$10.95."

Computed from `trades.db` (mode = `order_id LIKE 'PAPER-%'`):

- **PAPER** NO: 72.2% WR, **+$576** · YES: 27.4% WR, −$12.91
- **LIVE** NO: 25.2% WR, **−$17.51** · YES: 5.0% WR, −$56.07

The reported figure (~+$460, ~75% WR) matches the **paper** NO side at an earlier
date (it has since grown to +$576). It has never matched live. **Live NO money has
never been positive.** The entire "keep NO, it carries the bot" thesis rests on a
paper/live conflation.

## 2. Paper trading is not validation — it assumes away execution

`execution/executor.py`:
- **Paper entry (224–247):** order is marked `FILLED` instantly at the *quoted*
  `signal.kalshi_price`, full size, no slippage, no latency, no fill check.
- **Paper exit (812–844):** PnL = `(bid − entry) × contracts − fees`, instant, full size.

So paper is the backtest's fill assumption running forward. It assumes away the one
thing that kills the live strategy. Same period, same strategy: **paper +$544 vs
live −$11.** "Paper validated the signal" is circular — paper cannot validate
execution because it doesn't execute.

## 3. The backtester is invalid — two independent reasons

### 3a. Corrupt input data: massive crossed books with implied asks (affects BOTH engines)
`merged_backtest.db` `orderbook_snapshots` (the full engine's source). Share of rows
with `yes_bid + no_bid > 1.00` (an impossible state — both best bids cross, i.e. a
free-money arbitrage that real resting orders can't sustain → the stale-level bug):

| Month | Snapshots | Crossed |
|---|---|---|
| 2026-04 | 605,791 | **55.5%** |
| 2026-05 | 672,267 | **69.4%** |

Three verified facts make this fatal, not cosmetic:

1. **Asks are 100% implied.** All 1,162,530 rows satisfy `yes_ask = 1 − no_bid` and
   `no_ask = 1 − yes_bid` exactly. The recorder never stored an independent best-ask;
   it derived it from the *opposite bid*. So a stale, inflated bid directly produces a
   fake-cheap ask.
2. **The crossing is huge, not 1–2¢ noise.** `yes_bid + no_bid` runs to 1.7, 1.8, up
   to 2.00, across thousands of rows. Real recorded examples: `yes_bid=0.80,
   yes_ask=0.08`; `yes_bid=0.98, yes_ask=0.20`; `yes_bid=0.96, yes_ask=0.39`. A book
   that says "best bid to buy YES = 80¢, best offer to sell YES = 8¢" is not
   executable. (A 1–2¢ implied cross near 50/50 *is* normal Kalshi bounce — which is
   why commit `a27b357` "accept implied-crossed books, they're normal" looked
   reasonable. It is wrong at this magnitude: those 40–70¢ crosses are the bug.)
3. **This fakes win rate, not just dollars.** Full engine buys NO at
   `no_ask = 1 − yes_bid` (fake-cheap, e.g. 4¢ when yes_bid is a stale 0.96) and
   time-exits selling NO at `no_bid` (e.g. 61¢). Selling at a bid far above your
   entry ask books a profit **regardless of which way the market actually moved** —
   so both the WR and the PnL are manufactured.

The recorder logged this on **both sides of the May-23 trade-path fix**, so even
"clean era" backtest data is corrupted (May is *worse*: 69%). The full engine's
*design* is sound (real zero-lookahead guards, latency model); its *input* is not.
Garbage in, garbage out. (Note: I did not run the full engine or parse `runs.db`
`metrics_json`, so I can't quote its exact output — but with non-executable recorded
quotes, any output is untrustworthy by construction.)

### 3b. Look-ahead bias + fabricated payoff in the flex engine
`flex_engine.py` (source of the strategy-comparison table: combined $108K, mean
reversion $101K, NO-only, etc.), lines 217–235:
```python
won = (side=="yes" and result=="up") or (side=="no" and result=="down")  # settlement outcome
if time_exit_enabled and seconds_remaining > 90:
    if won: exit_value = entry_price + (1.0-entry_price)*0.6   # win: +60% of upside
    else:   exit_value = entry_price * 0.5                      # loss: only −50%
```
`time_exit` fires at T-30s but its PnL is computed from `won`, the **final settlement
result** — information that does not exist at exit time. This is textbook look-ahead.
The payoff is also fabricated and asymmetric (win big / lose half), giving every
trade a structurally winning expectation. This block *is* the $108K. The mean
reversion "94.9% WR" is the same artifact.

**Conclusion: no backtest number in this project is trustworthy.** The full engine is
the only salvageable tool, and only after the data is re-recorded clean.

feeds, paper-before-live discipline, A/B exit tests, walk-forward + bootstrap, and
extensive shadow logging. The skills are sound. **The failure is analytical, not
engineering:** decisions were steered by corrupted metrics and a paper/live mislabel.

## ADDENDUM — Paper performance re-analysis (2026-05-30)

Two more days of paper data (bot still live, PID 1972116). The cumulative "paper
+$544" is **two pre-fix days masquerading as an edge.** Splitting paper at the
May-23 crossed-book fix (commit `60dee18`):

| Paper era | Trades | WR | PnL | Per-trade |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PRE-fix (≤May 22)** | 324 | 65.1% | **+$529.88** | $1.64 |
| **POST-fix (May 24–30)** | 276 | 44.9% | **+$21.58** | **$0.08** |

The fix cut per-trade edge by **95%**. Pre-fix paper exits sold at impossible
inflated (crossed) bids — the *same* corruption as the backtest, running live. Once
books were trimmed, the edge evaporated. Post-fix paper is the only honest paper data,
and it is **flat**: cumulative drifted −1 → +30 → +21 over 7 days, peaking May 27 and
oscillating since. 276 trades to make $21 — under *perfect, zero-slippage, zero-latency,
full-size* fills.

Post-fix paper still shows the **settlement leak**: time_exit +$81 (54.7% WR) vs
settlement −$59 (2.2% WR). ~17% of positions leak past the T-30s exit to settlement
and die. This is a bug, not a choice — you can't trade "time_exit only," because
settlement *is* time_exit failing to fire.

**The one fair pro-edge signal:** isolating the intended config (NO-only + time_exit,
post-fix) — paper 131t, 54.2% WR, **+$0.38/trade**; live 17t, 58.8% WR, **+$0.34/trade**.
Paper and live finally *agree* here, both weakly positive. But (a) 17 live trades is
noise (90% CI on WR ≈ 40–77%), (b) it's inseparable from the −$59 settlement leak, and
(c) +$0.08–0.38/trade is the *ceiling* (perfect fills); real fills cost more than that,
which is why live net is still negative. Conclusion from §1–6 stands: no edge that
survives real execution.

## ADDENDUM 2 — Is the settlement leak fixable? (2026-05-30, clean-data counterfactual)

Tested whether an early exit could recover the settlement leak, on **clean post-fix
data, full population (winners + losers), zero look-ahead** — the inverse of the
flex-engine sin. For every entered position since May 24 (n=341, actual total
PnL = +$0.27), simulated exiting at the first recorded bid past a threshold, else
hold to actual outcome. Script: `scratch/stop_loss_counterfactual.py`.

| Overlay | Best threshold | Δ vs actual | Why it fails |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stop-loss** | all D | **−$1.82 to −$19.08** | cuts winners that dip & recover (at D=0.05, 105 of 299 stops were actual winners) |
| **Take-profit** | all G | **−$5.81 to −$24.13** | caps winners below their settle value; 147 winners locked early lose upside |

**Every threshold loses.** Mid-flight, winners and losers are indistinguishable:
winners dip to entry−0.15 then recover; losers spike above entry+0.05 then die. The
settlement leak (−$59 paper) is **intrinsic variance of binary contracts, not a bug.**
`time_exit` at <60s is the only overlay that helps, and it can't fire on losers
because their bid is already gone. **This vindicates the prior "don't re-enable
stop_loss/take_profit" call — but on valid evidence this time** (the old backtest that
"proved" it was itself corrupt). Conclusion: there is no exit-side fix that creates
edge. The losses are not a fixable bug; the signal simply lacks durable edge.

## ADDENDUM 3 — Edge-existence test (2026-05-30): IS there any edge at all?

Ran the gate test for v3 (`scratch/edge_existence_test.py`): on 1,280 clean post-fix
windows, do any features predict the outcome with edge beyond fees? No look-ahead
(features at the 91-480s decision point, outcome = actual settlement), no backtester.

**Result 1 — the market is efficient.** Realized P(up) tracks the entry price almost
perfectly: price 0.1→10.5%, 0.3→26%, 0.5→48%, 0.7→69%, 0.9→92.5%. The price *is* the
probability. There is no naive mispricing to harvest.

**Result 2 — no signal beats the price after fees.** Per-contract net edge (WR − price
− fee), needs net>0 and |z|>2:

| Signal | n | WR | price | gross | net | z |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| momentum sign (any) | 1145 | 54.7% | .558 | −.012 | −.028 | −0.8 |
| momentum > 0.0005 | 218 | 66.5% | .622 | +.043 | +.027 | +1.3 |
| momentum > 0.001 | 46 | 63.0% | .661 | −.030 | −.046 | −0.4 |
| momentum + OBI agree | 644 | 74.8% | .761 | −.013 | −.028 | −0.7 |
| **mom+OBI+price 0.25-0.65 (the bot)** | 153 | 51.0% | .560 | −.050 | **−.070** | −1.2 |
| model real_prob edge ≥ .04 | 977 | 23.4% | .249 | −.015 | −.031 | −1.1 |

Every signal is net-negative except `momentum>0.0005` (+.027) — and that one **fails
significance** (z=1.3, ~80% conf), **breaks monotonicity** (the stronger threshold
>0.001 reverses to −.046; a real edge would strengthen, not vanish), and is one of 6
cohorts tested (a z=1.3 by chance is expected). It is noise, not edge. The bot's
*actual* config is the single worst cohort (−.070). The dynamic-k model adds nothing
(its "edge≥4%" calls win at the priced rate).

**VERDICT: no exploitable edge in this signal family on this instrument.** The market
is efficiently priced; public microstructure features (momentum, OBI) are already in
the price; Kalshi's ~7% taker fee buries any residue. This is not a tuning problem.
v3 must change the **instrument** (longer horizon, less efficient market) or the
**role** (maker capturing spread, not taker paying it) — not the strategy. Reusing
momentum/OBI as a taker on 15-min crypto binaries is a proven dead end, now on valid
evidence.

## 7. Recommendations (decision-useful)

1. **Stop trusting every backtest and paper PnL number in the docs.** Treat the
   +$460 engine, the 90% WRs, and the $108K as retracted.
2. **Fix the data recorder first** (it still logs crossed books, and stores asks as
   implied `1 − opposite_bid` rather than the real book ask). Record the independent
   best ask, trim stale crossed levels at record time, re-record 2–3 weeks of clean
   data, *then* rebuild the backtest on the sound full engine. Until then you are
   flying blind. (Cheap sanity gate before trusting any future run: assert
   `< ~2%` of recorded snapshots have `yes_bid + no_bid > 1.02`.)
3. **Delete or quarantine `flex_engine.py`'s PnL path** — it is actively misleading
   (look-ahead). Do not use it for any decision.
4. **Chase the settlement leak, not latency.** 28 settlement losses (−$51) are the
   actual hole. FIX-protocol/AWS latency work is premature — you have no trustworthy
   evidence of an edge to capture, so optimizing capture is unjustified.
5. **Add durable provenance:** write `trading_mode` and the git commit hash into the
   `trades` and `sessions` rows. You currently cannot answer "what system placed this
   trade" from the schema alone.
6. **Honest EV:** 15-min crypto binaries are fee-heavy and near-efficient (retail vs
   market-makers). A small retail taker bot capturing durable edge is unlikely. The
   most probable truth is that the real edge is ~0 and the apparent edge was data
   corruption + idealized fills. One clean, small, live NO-only test *after* fixing
   the recorder and the settlement leak would settle it — but go in expecting ~0.
